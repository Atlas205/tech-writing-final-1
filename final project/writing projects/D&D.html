Dnd is one of if not the biggest roleplaying game with multitudes of ways to play using characters customization and different campaigns which make it so it's very different each time you play


For the characters you have everything from regular humans to dwarves, elves and even dragonborn each one adds its own features from boosting your stats too giving racial features Humans boost all stats by one unless you're using variant which boosts one by two and two by one while also giving a feat which I will get into later Dwarves give a bonus to constitution by two and depending on the subrace another bonus they also give racial features such as dwarven resilience which boosts your health by one whenever you level up Elves give bonuses to dexterity being plus two and charisma plus one they also do not need to sleep in game they only need to meditate and they cannot be put to sleep with spells and they get dark vision which acts like night vision goggles with no color Dragonborn boost your constitution by 1 and strength by 2 and the racial feature is a breath weapon which is an ability that you can use once per long rest which depending on your dragon's color can be ice and fire and even poison

There are even more races but that’s too many to cover now we can move unto stats



The main stats are constitution which affects your health how well you can defend against poison and also how well you can drink, dexterity affects how well you can move and how precise you can move but also your hit chance with a ranged weapon, strength affects how much you can carry how much you can move and how much damage you can do with a sword, wisdom is your ability to tell where architecture comes from or just good judgement, intelligence is how much you know how well you can use certain spells, charisma is how well you can convince people of something be it making a guard let you pass by complimenting them or threatening them or any other way you can convince people


For the rest of character customization there's the classes which have the biggest effect on how you play there's a bunch so I will go over the most used ones

Fighters are the most commonly used class being also one of the simplest to play as they do not have any magic abilities as they tend to rely on their main weapon and their ability to have the multi attack feature Rogues are one of the most broken classes because they have a ton of features for lessening the damage they take from attacks and they have sneak attack which gives a bonus to attack depending on your level Warlocks are cultists they worship dark gods such as the fiend or the old one which give them features depending on which one you choose and they get magic which tends to be very powerful but they can only use it at their max level which means you run out of uses fast but deal a lot of damage

There's plenty of more but I recommend researching those on your own




Now the campaigns there's plenty of campaigns to choose from which can range from easy to nigh impossible depending on which one you're running such as an example of the one of the hardest campaigns would be tomb of horrors which players and dms tend to refer to it as a meat grinder its full of tricks and traps which will most likely get you unless you’ve memorized the entire campaign by heart but there's also easier campaigns such as the one that comes with the starter set



Spells are a core feature in dnd being used by almost every class with few exceptions they can range from the cantrips which are the weakest but you can use almost infinitely all the up to the most powerful ninth level spells which can change the game completely such as the wish spell which does what it says it grants the players wish but it can have a twist depending on the DM
